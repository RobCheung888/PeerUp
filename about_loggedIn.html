About

Because of COVID many people are suffering from mental illnesses

The COVID-19 pandemic and the resulting economic recession have negatively affected many people’s mental health and created new barriers for people already with mental illness and substance use disorders. About 4 in 10 adults in the U.S. have reported anxiety or depressive disorder symptoms during the pandemic. This share has been broadly consistent, up from one in ten adults who reported these symptoms from January to June 2019.

The Impacts of COVID-19 on mental health

On average, before the pandemic from January - June 2019, around 11% of adults had reported symptoms of an anxiety disorder and/or a dperessive disorder. In just two years, that number has almost quadrupled. A more recent study in January 2021, shows that this number has jumped to over 40%. This shows the severity of the COVID-19 pandemic and the negative impact it is making on people’s mental health. It is important to keep yourself enetrtained during these tough times and take good care of our mental health. If you need any tips on how to keep yourself enetrtained during this pandemic feel free to visit our tips page which you can access in the navigation bar.

Why is this important?

All of this infomration is quite important as it opens up our eyes to how easy it is to develop a mental disorder. It’s good to be enlightened about this information so you can prevent a mental disorder. In addition, it is also important to know that if you are suffering from anxiety and/or depression you are not alone. Many people out there are also fighting the same battles as you and its okay to feel that way. If you feel like you do not have anyone to talk to, and want to open up to someone, you can use PeerUp. And don’t worry about feeling embarassed opening up to someone you just met online, because we will keep all your information anonymous!
